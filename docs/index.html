Motivation

Why video-caption-QA is not enough?

Existing pipelines often split videos into short clips, caption audio and vision separately, and generate QA directly from long descriptions.

While highly scalable, this paradigm weakens the deep temporal connections and multimodal interactions that complex audio-visual comprehension demands. OmniVideo-100K is designed to address three key problems in current data generation pipelines.

Problem 01

Broken Audio-Visual Associations

Separate audio and visual descriptions sever the inherent association between a sound, its visual source, and their temporal synchronization.

Problem 02

Incoherent Visual Narratives

Independent segment captions often cause inconsistent entity references across segments, making it difficult for LLMs to maintain coherent narratives.

Problem 03

Localized QA Synthesis

Single-pass QA generation from long captions frequently restricts questions to localized events, failing to capture long-term temporal connections and complex cross-modal synergy.

Contribution

Structured Scripts

Transform videos into structured scripts comprising summaries, main entity lists, timestamped audio, speaker labels, and coherent visual narratives.

Contribution

Clue-Guided QA Generation

Prompt models to mine cross-segment and cross-modality clues, and then construct QA pairs anchored to these evidence chains.

Contribution

100K Train & Verified Test

Build OmniVideo-100K for instruction tuning, alongside OmniVideo-Test, a 505-sample test set manually verified for cross-modal dependency.

Method

Entity-anchored scripts & Clue-guided QA

The pipeline formalizes the raw video into a structured text representation, enabling LLMs to mine evidence chains before QA synthesis.

01Main Entity List

Identify primary active entities in the raw video and assign unique identifiers to establish a global prior.

02Video Summary

Generate a narrative summary of the raw video, with entity references constrained by the Main Entity List.

03Timestamped Audio

Transcribe speech timestamps from the audio stream, and record non-speech sounds and music descriptions.

04Speaker Labeling

Tag speech with speakers by analyzing the raw video and referencing the Main Entity List to link modalities.

05Video Segmentation

Partition the raw video into segments based on generated speech timestamp boundaries.

06Visual Narrative

Generate segment-wise visual captions from the video frames, using the Main Entity List to keep references consistent.

07Global Clue Mining

Scan the integrated script to extract cross-modal and cross-segment clues to build task-specific evidence chains.

08Locally Focused QA

Construct QA pairs by focusing model on the relevant video segments and mined clues.

Entity-Anchored Video Scripting

Transform raw videos into entity-anchored scripts, comprising a summary, a main entity list, and structured segment-wise descriptions that integrate speech, sounds, and visual information. Using consistent identifiers (orange and green text) from the main entity list ensures narrative coherence across different segments and associates speech content with visual entities.

Clue-Guided QA Generation

Prompt LLMs to mine cross-segment and cross-modal clues from the script, then produce QA pairs based on the clues and relevant segments.

Evidence Chain
Question

Why is Brunette Girl happy with her blind box?

Visual clue

Earlier [03:34 - 03:44], another person is shown holding a Pop Mart doll that carries a red soda can. Later [07:17 - 07:31], the scene shows the Brunette Girl holding her own newly unboxed doll, which carries a brown soda bottle labeled Coca-Cola.

Audio clue

At [03:34 - 03:39], upon seeing another doll being shown, Brunette Girl expresses her expectation: "I hope I get one with a coke bottle instead of a can though." Later, at [07:17 - 07:25], she opens her own blind box and happily exclaims: "I love it."

Answer

It is holding a bottle instead of a can.

Video Curation
  • 1Collect online videos and iteratively expand the search keyword pool from seven core labels.
  • 3Exclude videos with resolutions below 480p and retain only English videos.
  • 4Apply visual dynamics and word-density filters to keep videos rich in audio-visual information.
  • 5Remove videos with hard-coded subtitles to reduce shortcut learning from on-screen text.
OmniVideo-Test Quality control
  • 1Remove QA pairs that contain factual errors or ambiguous multiple-choice options.
  • 2Discard QA pairs that can be answered or easily guessed using only a single modality.
  • 3Confirm answer uniqueness to guarantee that only one option is unambiguously correct.
  • 4Conclude the manual screening with a final acceptance rate of approximately 38.14%.

Case studies

From vague guesses to grounded evidence chains

Each case highlights how audio and visual evidence jointly resolve ambiguities that baseline models miss.

Question

During the discussion, the bald pundit reaches up and removes the glasses resting on his forehead. What is he speculating about at that exact moment?

Baseline failure

Qwen2.5-Omni gives a vague guess that he may be making a point or trying to see better, without grounding the action in the spoken discussion.

Audio evidence

The speech mentions that the club may get a decision in the coming weeks because they are complaining.

Visual evidence

The glasses-removal gesture occurs at the same time as the pundit discusses the club contacting officials over the decision.

Question

What specific detail in the narrative motivated the speaker's gesture near his head?

Baseline failure

Qwen2.5-Omni focuses on the nearby phrase about the father being a soldier and turns the gesture into a broad emotional interpretation.

Audio evidence

The speaker recalls a man returning with a deep dent in his military helmet.

Visual evidence

The speaker raises his hand near his head while describing the dented helmet, aligning the gesture with the specific helmet detail.

Question

What specific aspect of the interview motivated the man in blue to advise that players need to act to avoid predators?

Baseline failure

Qwen2.5-Omni reduces predators to general expectations and scrutiny, missing the prior admission and the interview dynamics.

Audio evidence

The man in black admits that he has been terrible and says players must not let their guard down because predators are watching.

Visual evidence

The man in blue nods and gestures emphatically, showing that his advice is tied to the other speaker's admission and the interview context.
